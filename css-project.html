My Master Thesis: Exploring the Path to Breaking Poverty Lines

Posted on Saturday, January 20, 2025

The Inspiration Behind the Thesis

Education is often referred to as the great equalizer, a tool capable of breaking poverty cycles and enabling upward social mobility. Yet, the reality is far from ideal in many parts of the world, including Mexico. Growing up in a country with stark socioeconomic disparities, I became acutely aware of the challenges millions face in accessing quality education. These challenges sparked my curiosity: How could we create a system that provides equal opportunities for all?

The Core Focus

My thesis, “(In-)Equality of Opportunity in the Mexican Education System: A Survey- and Literature-Based Evaluation,” examined inequality of opportunity within Mexico’s education system. Specifically, it sought to identify how socioeconomic factors—such as family income, geographic location, and parental education—impact a child’s ability to access quality education and, ultimately, break free from poverty.

Key Findings

  • Socioeconomic Disparities: Family background remains one of the strongest determinants of educational success. Students from wealthier families consistently outperform their peers from low-income households, not due to a lack of ability, but due to unequal access to resources.
  • Geographic Inequalities: Rural areas face significant challenges, including fewer schools, poorly trained teachers, and limited access to technological resources. These gaps widen the divide between urban and rural educational outcomes.
  • Policy Gaps: While some policies aim to improve education equity, many fall short in addressing the root causes of inequality, such as poverty and social exclusion. A more holistic approach is necessary.

The Broader Implications

This research reinforced a critical understanding: education reform alone is not enough. To genuinely break poverty lines, policymakers must address the interconnected factors of inequality, such as income disparity, social mobility, and access to healthcare. Only then can education fulfill its promise of providing equal opportunities for all.

A Personal Reflection

Writing this thesis was both challenging and rewarding. It was an opportunity to contribute to a pressing issue close to my heart, and it deepened my understanding of the systemic changes required to achieve true equality of opportunity. More importantly, it solidified my commitment to advocating for policies that prioritize education equity, not just in Mexico, but globally.
